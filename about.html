About

Claire O'Neill


Data Science and Computer Science student at The University of Michigan

I am currently a computer science and data science student at the University of Michigan, who is passionate about using technology to address climate change. I believe in using technology to help improve the world, using it as a tool to address complex problems that I feel most passionate about. Using my skills as a data scientist, I have been able to complete a project that exposed the impacts of plastic pollution, using tableau to visualize the impacts on a global scale.

Professionally, I continue to work as a data science intern for IT Concepts, where my team is developing a tool to reduce toxic language in corporate documents. This work has allowed me to develop skills in natural language processing and machine learning. I was able to train a transformer to detect hate speech which performed at an 88% accuracy. As I continue to work on this project, I hope to continue to strengthen my skills as a software developer as well as a data scientist.

In my free time I enjoy listening to music, working on personal projects, and continuing to develop my skills in graphic design. I currently serve as the president and treasurer of the Michigan Intercollegiate Polo Club, where I am able to apply my data science skills while managing our budget using Excel. I had the opportunity to organize a merchandise fundraiser that generated over $1,000 in revenue, allowing me to use my graphic design skills to design and market merchandise.

I am an incredibly ambitious student, with a unique background that has given me a wide array of skills that have prepared me to be successful in data science and software engineering roles alike. I hope to continue to expand on my current skill sets through future projects and work opportunities. I am a passionate student who is excited to use my skills to make an impact.
